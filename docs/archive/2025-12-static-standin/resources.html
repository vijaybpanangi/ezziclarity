Resources & Frequently Asked Questions

A starting point for some of the most common questions we hear from students and families.

FAQs

When should we reach out for help?

Any time you feel stuck, overloaded, or uncertain. Earlier is better, but it is never too late to bring structure to your situation.

Do you replace school or institutional advisors?

No. We work alongside existing advisors by helping you clarify your goals, organize information, and ask better questions so that you can use institutional resources more effectively.

Do you guarantee admissions or outcomes?

We do not guarantee admissions or specific outcomes. We focus on improving decision quality, reducing avoidable mistakes, and helping you move with a clear plan.

Can you work with us if we are outside Canada?

In many cases yes, especially for planning and clarity work. We will be transparent about where we can add value and where local expertise is required.

Planning prompts

If you are preparing for a session with us, or simply want to think more clearly on your own, these questions can help:

  • What are your top three priorities for the next 12â24 months?
  • What are you currently unsure or conflicted about?
  • What constraints are non negotiable (time, budget, location, obligations)?
  • What assumptions are you making that might deserve a second look?
  • What would a good outcome look like a year from now?

How to get the most from a Clarity Session

  1. Come with your questions and concerns written down.
  2. Share relevant documents or background information in advance where possible.
  3. Be open about constraints, including the ones that feel awkward to mention.
  4. Ask follow up questions if anything is unclear or feels too abstract.
  5. After the session, schedule 30â60 minutes to review your notes and next steps.
